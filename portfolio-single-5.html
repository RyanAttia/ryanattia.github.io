3D Scene Reconstruction Using Stereo Images and Motion Capture Data

Project Date Oct, 2023
Role Computer Vision, Machine Learning

This project focused on accurately reconstructing a 3D environment by fusing data from stereo image pairs and motion capture (MoCap) systems. The goal was to build a scalable and precise 3D model by combining geometric computer vision techniques with real-world motion tracking data. I began by calibrating the stereo camera setup using known intrinsic and extrinsic parameters, which allowed for accurate projection of MoCap markers onto the stereo image planes. I validated these projections against ground truth to ensure spatial consistency and alignment across frames.

To derive depth and reconstruct 3D points, I implemented triangulation algorithms, allowing for spatial measurement of scene elements such as floor and wall planes. I then computed the Fundamental matrix using the 8-point algorithm, and evaluated the geometric integrity of the reconstruction by measuring the Symmetric Epipolar Distance between feature correspondences and epipolar lines. Throughout the project, I developed well-documented MATLAB scripts for each pipeline stage—from calibration and projection to triangulation and validation—ensuring modularity and reproducibility. The result was a robust 3D reconstruction workflow, capable of integrating physical MoCap data with stereo vision to model complex scenes with high spatial accuracy.
